About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at Sandia National Laboratories, affiliated with the University of Utah and a proud alumnus of IIT Kanpur. My work sits at the crossroads of computational chemistry, materials science, and machine learning.

With over 6 years of experience, I specialize in molecular dynamics (MD) simulations, free energy calculations, and density functional theory (DFT). Throughout my career I have been deeply immersed in energy and environmental sciences ‚Äî contributing to the development of efficient polymer electrolytes for batteries and innovative solutions for water pollutant removal, with a particular focus on PFAS.

I have extensively collaborated with experimentalists while working on a wide range of materials, from surfactants and polymers to earth minerals. More recently, I have ventured into machine learning, developing models to accurately predict the physical and chemical properties of molecules, and utilizing generative models such as constrained graph variational autoencoders to design novel molecules with enhanced efficiency.

You can find my full list of publications on my Google Scholar profile and connect with me on LinkedIn.
